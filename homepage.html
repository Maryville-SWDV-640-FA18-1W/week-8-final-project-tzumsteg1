About Coach Zumsteg

Hello! I'm Tim Zumsteg and I'm a teacher at Washington High School in Washington, Missouri. I currently teach Dicrete Mathematics, Geometry, and Pre-Algebra. I also coach JV Baseball and Softball at Washington High. I was born and raised in Washington, Missouri and went to Washington High School where I graduated in 2011. After High school I attended East Central College in Union, Missouri where I used my A+ money to complete my General Eduacation classes. I then transferred to the University of Central Missouri in 2013. I student taught at Holt High School in Wentzville, Missouri. I went on to graduate from UCM with a BSE in Mathematics in 2016. Just before Graduation, I accepted my first teaching job with the School District of Washington. I am currently working on my Master's Degree in Software Management through Maryville University in St. Louis, Missouri. I will have my degree in 2019.

Classes I Teach

Discrete Mathematics

1 Semester (Fall or Spring)

This course is intended for students who are interested in, but not limited to degrees in computer science and the social sciences. The content will focus on contemporary topics in mathematics including number theory, voting methods, cryptology, networking, logic, number systems, optimization, cyber security, statistics and probability

Geometry

Year-Long Course

This course is a study of plane Euclidean geometry including proofs. The student will use deductive reasoning and logic to draw conclusions when given certain facts. A study of geometric shapes and figures, congruence and similarity, area, volume, constructions, plane and three-dimensional figures and their theorems, postulates and definitions are included

Pre-Algebra

Year-Long Course

This course is designed for students not yet ready for Algebra and will focus on developing students’ understanding of mathematics. Topics covered will include operations on rational numbers, simplifying algebraic expressions, manipulating formulas, solving linear equations and inequalities, graphing linear equations and inequalities, proportions, problem solving, and elementary statistics and probability. It is recommended that students successfully completing PreAlgebra continue with Algebra I Concepts
